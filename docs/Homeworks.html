#5.

Find expression: ([A-Z])[a-z]+,([a-z]+),.?()

Let’s break down what this regex does: ([A-Z]) captures just the first capital letter of the genus name [a-z]+ matches the rest of the genus name but doesn’t capture it , matches the comma ([a-z]+) captures the species name , matches the comma .?atches the decimal number (but doesn’t capture it) , matches the comma () captures the final number

In the replacement pattern: \1 puts the first letter of the genus _ adds the underscore \2 puts the species name , keeps the comma \3 puts the final number

Replace expression: \1_\2,\3

In the replacement pattern:

\1 puts the first letter of the genus _ adds the underscore \2 puts the first 4 letters of the species name , keeps the comma \3 puts the final number

This will transform your data into the exact format requested, with abbreviated genus and species names.

Replace expression: \1_\2,\3
